Spellbound


Find the website here: Spellbound

Description: This project was for my Human-Computer Interaction class. We worked in teams of 4 to needfind an idea, develop low and medium fidelity UI prototypes using paper and Figma, perform heuristic evaluations and UX tests, and finally code a semi-functional high fidelity version. Our project was Spellbound, a “voice-interactive” spelling app to help kids learn in a fun and engaging way.

My main contributions included initial needfinding, drawing the low-fi prototype, helping design the medium-fi prototype, conducting UX research, and implementing the project’s website.

A highly detailed version of our journey through this project, including a working medium-fi prototype, can be found in the link above.

Awards won: Best Concept and Best Overall Project at the Stanford 2018 HCI Expo

Technologies Pencil and paper, Figma, HTML & CSS, Swift, UX/UI Design
